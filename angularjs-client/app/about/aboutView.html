Education

In 2015 I completed my Computer Systems Technology diploma at the British Columbia Institute of Technology and specialized in Web and Mobile development. I plan to continue on to the degree program at BCIT in the Network Security and Administration Option

My main focus of my studies in the diploma program was backend development and the exploration of RESTful services and system management. This included development in PHP and ASP.NET where I developed WebApps and RESTful API's using a number of frameworks. I also explored front-end development with Android, iOS and some web development. I have become an avid fan of the Nodejs engine and Angularjs framework for its amazing power and intricacy it adds to a typical website and the development process.

Additionally in my studies, I have worked with Java, C, and C++. One of my first projects I created on my own was a Chess simulator that was developed in Java. This simple yet somewhat complicated project taught me alot about inheritance and polymorphism. The project was large enough also to be an example of the value of code re-use and to develope with code re-use in mind. With Java being the first language I was taught while attending BCIT, many of my coding 'best practices' are based on standards enforced by this language and its developers. During my second and third semester at BCIT I used C and C++ to make various console utilities including a grocery store simulator in C++ and a number of file manipulating tools in C.

While at BCIT I was also accepted into Co-op for my program and had an 8 month internship at Cirius Secure Messaging as a Quality Assurance Analyst. Periodically though I had the opportunity to do some development on some internal tools. Along with learning C# from ASP.NET at BCIT, I also gained valuable experience and learned a large amount about C# while at Cirius.
